Process

Design is Collaborative & Hands-on

Background

  • My Design Toolkit

After spending four years studying HCI at UC San Diego, I've had a lot of time to work and refine my design process.

I pride myself on my strong enthusiasm for turning design-thinking into design-doing, and I truly believe that working towards these technological opportunities ultimately starts with discovering, defining, developing, and delivering.


Contextual Interviews: While developing Soundnova for a class project, I conducted contextual interviews while users completed tasks using SoundCloud and Spotify.

Focus Groups: As an intern at Workday, I organized focus groups to determine insights about users who would be using the data visualizations and wearable tech I designed.

Surveys: Using social media outlets on Facebook, LinkedIn, and Twitter, I created and sent out online surveys to scope interest during the initial need-finding for Brandpackers.

Discovering through

  • Contextual Interviews
  • Focus Groups
  • Surveys

In the first step of the design process, we can discover insights into the problem through proper empathy, user research, and understanding.

Due to my coursework in ethnography and field research, I have working experience in grounded research methods. My passion for placing people before technology pushes me to discover more about the communities around.

Value Flow Models: While leading an ideation session, I drew up value flow models while trying to design the university recruiting experience for Brandpackers.

Affinity Diagrams: While defining common problems for teachers using edtech, I created an affinity diagram based on interview findings during my internship at Learning Equality.

Heuristic Evaluation: I wrote up a detailed heuristic evaluation on BrainBuilders for my research project at The Design Lab, closely following Nielsen‚Äôs Ten Heuristics.

Sitemaps: While conducting a competitive analysis of popular music streaming apps, I created sitemaps for the 3rd level navigations of YouTube, Spotify, and SoundCloud.

User Storyboards & Scenarios: While developing Brandpackers for class, my team and I created several storyboards to represent user scenarios for both students and recruiters.

Customer Journey Maps: During my time at The Design Lab, my team and I drew up a customer journey map from a user testing session we observed with the BrainBuilder app.

Defining through

  • Value Flow Models
  • Affinity Diagrams
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • Sitemaps
  • User Storyboards & Scenarios
  • Customer Journey Maps

After gathering more information about our users, we can then define the problem using various models, affinity diagrams, storyboards, and user personas.

After extensive research and review, converging on key user insights is critical for thinking about solutions that will hit the mark and solve the right user frustrations.

Whiteboarding Sessions: Working together with a fellow UX intern, I spearheaded whiteboarding sessions for how to design Kolibri's coach report dashboard.

Participatory Design: While redesigning a website for a frozen yogurt caf√©, my team and I worked together with our client to pinpoint opportunities for change.

Parallel Prototyping: While working to redesign the family experience at MOPA, I used parallel prototyping to explore 3 different solutions for our design opportunity.

Wireframes: For my interaction design class, my team and I worked together to create the screens and wireframes for Brandpackers, our university recruiting startup.

Workflows: As the interaction design lead for Soundnova, I developed two separate workflows for adding to a music playlist and revising an existing playlist.

Paper Prototyping: For a social productivity app I designed for class, my team and I first sketched out paper prototypes of our potential screens on iOS.

Developing through

  • Ideation Sessions
  • Participatory Design
  • Parallel Prototyping
  • Wireframes
  • Workflows
  • Paper Prototyping

Once we have defined the problem at hand, we can develop potential solutions using ideation and rapid prototyping.

As a designer, it's important to consider all possible opportunities before building your interface. For this reason, I'm a strong believer in parallel prototyping multiple solutions at once.

Visual Design: As the visual design lead in my Brandpackers project, I spearheaded the consistency of our branding and design for our proposed startup using Sketch 3 and Zeplin.

Rapid Prototyping: For my intern project on Workday‚Äôs Google Nearby integration, I used Sketch and Principle to create animations for various workflows.

User Testing: For an iOS application I produced in a class on HCI, I conducted task-based usability testing for the unlabeled elements and iconography of my project.

Delivering through

  • Visual Design
  • Rapid Prototyping
  • User Testing

Finally, we can deliver refined solutions that hit the mark through thorough user testing, concept validation, and iteration.

Due to my experience with front-end development and prototyping tools, I work efficiently to implement interactions on a micro- and macro-level.

The Truth: The design process is messy and gets everywhere.

Actual Process

  • It's Not Perfect

In reality, the design process is never this clean. Sometimes, it takes more research than development, and sometimes it takes more defining to deliver.


But through this design process, it definitely becomes a lot more coherent.
